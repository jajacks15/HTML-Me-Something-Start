One Piece: The Wano-Kuni arc retold

One Piece is a weekly manga series by Eiichiro Oda, which has published and serialized in Shueisha's Weekly Shonen Jump magazine since 1997. Since serialization, One Piece has published a total of 1109 chapters which are compiled in 108 volumes. The manga received an anime adaptation in October 1999 and has been airing on television ever since, with a current total of 1096 episodes and counting.

At the end of December 2023, we saw the conclusion of One Piece's latest mega arc, Wano-kuni, which brought an end to the story's "New World" saga. On this page, you will see a retelling of the story of Wano-kuni, with a brief synopsis of the story thus far and relevasnt context to the climactic saga. Please enjoy!

Luffy (center) and his Straw Hat Pirate crew

Context

One Piece is the story of a pirate boy named Monkey D. Luffy. Luffy's goal is to gather a pirate crew who will help him sail the seas of the Grand Line and ultimately find the One Piece, the treasure left behind by the king of the pirates - Gol D. Roger. Doing so would make Luffy the new kiong of the pirates and help him achieve his dream, but the Grand Line is a harsh sea which presents many difficult challenges to Luffy. Luffy has since collected his crew-- the Strawhat Pirates-- and they are taking the Grand Line by storm.

Leading up to the Wano-kuni arc, Luffy and the Straw-hat pirates have made quite a name for themselves. They have visited uncharted territories, liberated countries that have been terrorized by pirates identified by the World Government as "Warlords of the Sea", they've learned a bit about the history of the world they live in, and they've even picked a fight with two of the pirates who hold the title of "The Four Emperors of the Sea". Luffy is a member of a young group of new pirates who are making big waves in the Grand Line, called "The Worst Generation". He has formed an alliance with one of the members of that group, named Trafalger Law, to take down King of the Beasts, Kaido, one of the four Emperors. On the island that they form the alliance with Law and his pirate group, the Heart Pirates, the Straw Hat crew meets two strange samurai-- a man named Kin'emon and a young child named Momonosuke who join the crew's adventure in hopes of finding their samurai friends who they've been separated from. They eventually reunite with their lost samurai friends and learn that they share the common goal of defeating Kaido in their home country, Wano.

The groups set sail to Wano, determined to bring down Emperor Kaido, but there is also an important second goal, to find a missing piece of the map to the One Piece, which Luffy and his crew are ever so close to finding. Will they achieve their goals of defeating Kaido and finding the missing piece??

Arriving at Wano

Wano's Flower Capital, the only well-maintained land under Orochi and Kaido's regime

As Luffy lands on the shores of Wano and meets a young girl named Otama, nicknamed "Tama". Through Tama, Luffy learns that the once beautiful land of Wano has been diminished to an enfamished wasteland with poisoned waters by Kaido. The only place in Wano that has kept its former glory is the Flower Capital, which is where the evil new shogun, Orochi lives.

Wano Act One

Kaido in his newly revealed dragon form

Because Tama gives Luffy the only rice that she gets to eat in the entire year, Luffy vows to defeat Kaido no matter what to restore Wano back to what it was. Luffy and his crew learns that Kin'emon and his samurai companions travelled from 20 years in the past to challenge Kaido to a decisive battle with the remaining samurai in his group that are scattered across Wano. Despite learning that Kaido can transform into a dragon, Luffy challenges Kaido to a one-on-one duel but loses badly and is thrown in jail.

Wano Act Two

Kozuki Oden, the former shogun of Wano

While in jail, Luffy meets another one of the Worst Generation, Eustace Kidd, who has also been defeated by Kaido. They begin to compete against each other to get stronger and break out of prison. We learn the Momonosuke is the son of Kozuki Oden, the former shogun of Wano. Momnonosuke will rule as the new shogun should they defeat Kaido. Kin'emon eventually meets up with the remaining samurai in his group and the team prepares to set sail towads Kaido and challenge him to take back Wano.

Wano Act Three

Kaido (left) and Luffy (right) during their final confrontation

In Act Three of Wano, Luffy, the allied pirates, the samurai, and other allies battle Kaido, his pirate crew, and the evil shogun Orochi. In the middle of the battle, Momonosuke ages 20 years using the magic of one of the samurai and he helps Luffy defeat Kaido. Orochi is also defeated and Momonosuke is able to take his rightful spot as shogun of Wano.

Story Implications

Upon defeating Kaido and Big Mom-- another Emperor who followed Luffy into Wano-- Luffy has been named as one of the new Four Emperors. The Straw Hats also find the missing piece of the missing map to the One Piece. There is now one piece (no pun intended) of the map that the crew needs in order to finally locate the king of the pirates' treasure.
